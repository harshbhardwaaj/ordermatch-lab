## WRITING RULES

1. NEVER use: synergy, leverage (verb), passionate, thrilled, excited to apply, detail-oriented, team player, hardworking, results-driven, dynamic, innovative, spearheaded, utilized, impactful, cutting-edge
2. NEVER use these AI vocabulary words: additionally, showcase, testament, underscore, vibrant, pivotal, crucial, delve, fostering, align with, landscape (abstract), tapestry, intricate, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, enduring, valuable, enhance, emphasizing
3. NEVER use significance inflation: "serves as", "stands as", "marks a pivotal moment", "represents a shift", "is a testament to", "underscores its importance". Use "is" or just state the fact.
4. NEVER use -ing padding phrases tacked onto sentences: "highlighting X", "ensuring Y", "contributing to Z", "underscoring the importance of". Cut them.
5. NEVER use negative parallelisms: "It's not just X — it's Y", "Not only X, but Y", "It's not about X, it's about Z". State the point directly.
6. NEVER use filler: "In order to", "it is important to note that", "at this point in time", "due to the fact that", "has the ability to". Cut or compress.
7. NEVER use generic positive closings: "exciting times lie ahead", "the future looks bright", "I look forward to contributing". Close with availability and a specific reason.
8. NEVER use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–). Use commas or periods instead.
9. Every bullet starts with a strong verb: Built, Designed, Deployed, Screened, Modeled, Conducted, Wrote, Led, Managed, Evaluated, Delivered
10. Specific metrics where they naturally exist — never force fake numbers
11. Bullets: 1-2 lines max
12. Cover letter: 3 short paragraphs only — opening / main story / closing + availability
13. Every bullet string must start with "• " (bullet U+2022 + space)
14. INTERESTS is a plain comma-separated string, not a list
